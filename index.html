Immediate Safety Protocols

Critical actions for active threat situations

Life-Threatening Situation Protocol:
  1. Move to predetermined safe room/shelter
  2. Contact security officer via encrypted channel
  3. Activate team alert system
  4. Follow evacuation protocol if necessary
  5. Document situation when safe

Active Shooter/Armed Attack

▼

RUN - HIDE - FIGHT Protocol

RUN: If safe evacuation route exists

  • Leave belongings behind
  • Help others escape if possible
  • Prevent others from entering danger area
  • Call security forces when safe

HIDE: If evacuation not possible

  • Lock/barricade doors
  • Turn off lights
  • Silence phones
  • Hide behind large objects
  • Remain quiet

FIGHT: Last resort if life in imminent danger

  • Act aggressively
  • Improvise weapons
  • Commit to your actions

Bombing/Explosion

▼

During Explosion

  • Drop to ground immediately
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture
  • Cover head and neck with arms
  • Stay down until shaking stops

After Explosion

  • Check for injuries
  • Exit building if safe (watch for secondary devices)
  • Avoid windows and glass
  • Don't use elevators
  • Account for team members
  • Move to rally point

Kidnapping/Abduction

▼

Prevention

  • Vary routes and schedules
  • Maintain low profile
  • Avoid predictable patterns
  • Travel in groups when possible

If Taken

  • Stay calm and alert
  • Comply with demands initially
  • Observe surroundings for intel
  • Maintain dignity without antagonizing
  • Prepare mentally for long-term captivity
  • Look for escape opportunities

Evacuation Protocols

Structured withdrawal and emergency evacuation procedures

Phase 1: Pre-Evacuation Alert

Security situation deteriorating. Prepare for possible evacuation. Update documents, charge devices, prepare go-bags.

Phase 2: Evacuation Warning

Evacuation likely within 48 hours. Secure sensitive materials, backup data, notify partners.

Phase 3: Evacuation Order

Immediate evacuation. Move to assembly point, follow predetermined routes, maintain communication.

Phase 4: Post-Evacuation

Safe location reached. Debrief, medical checks, contact families, begin recovery process.

Trauma & Psychological Support

Mental health resources for war zone stress and trauma

Normal Reactions to Abnormal Situations: Experiencing trauma symptoms in war zones is a normal response. Seeking help is a sign of wisdom, not weakness.

Common Trauma Responses

  • Hypervigilance and startle response
  • Sleep disturbances and nightmares
  • Emotional numbing or detachment
  • Intrusive memories or flashbacks
  • Survivor guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, fatigue)

Immediate Coping Strategies

  • Grounding: 5-4-3-2-1 sensory technique
  • Breathing: Box breathing (4-4-4-4)
  • Movement: Physical exercise daily
  • Connection: Regular team check-ins
  • Routine: Maintain daily structure
  • Limits: Reduce news consumption

Security Protocols

Comprehensive security guidelines for conflict zones

Personal Security Measures

▼
  • Maintain low profile - avoid logos, flags
  • Vary daily routines and routes
  • Establish check-in schedules
  • Know local emergency numbers
  • Identify safe havens and hospitals
  • Keep vehicle fueled above 50%
  • Maintain 2-week supply of essentials

Residential Security

▼
  • Install security film on windows
  • Reinforce doors with deadbolts
  • Create safe room with supplies
  • Install motion sensor lights
  • Keep curtains drawn at night
  • Establish neighborhood watch
  • Test alarm systems regularly

Digital Security

▼
  • Use VPN for all internet access
  • Enable 2-factor authentication
  • Regular encrypted backups
  • Secure deletion of sensitive data
  • Avoid public WiFi networks
  • Use encrypted storage devices
  • Regular security audits

How to Use This App

Purpose

This application provides comprehensive support resources for global workers serving in war zones and conflict areas. It consolidates security protocols, trauma care, evacuation procedures, and member care resources.

Navigation

  • Use the side menu to access different sections
  • On mobile, tap the menu button to show/hide navigation
  • Each section contains specific resources and tools

Key Features

  • Immediate Safety: Crisis response protocols
  • Evacuation Plans: Step-by-step procedures
  • Trauma Care: Mental health support
  • Secure Communication: Encrypted channels
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluation tools

Emergency Use

In crisis situations, go directly to "Immediate Safety" or "Evacuation Plans" sections for critical protocols.
